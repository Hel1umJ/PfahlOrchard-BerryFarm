Produce Variety & Ripening Schedule

Apples

Our main crop, we currently have 6 acres of apple trees planted with over 300 trees already planted. We have a variety of apples planted, catered towards both eating and cider production.

Liberty

Taste: Sweet-tart flavor with crisp, juicy texture. Excellent for fresh eating with good storage quality.

Ripening: Mid to late September

Notes: Disease-resistant variety, great for organic growing

Honeycrisp

Taste: Exceptionally crisp and juicy with a perfect sweet-tart balance. The gold standard for fresh eating apples.

Ripening: Mid to late September

Notes: Stores well and maintains crispness longer than most varieties

Dolgo (Cider)

Taste: Very tart and astringent, not suitable for fresh eating. Produces deep red, full-bodied cider with complex flavor.

Ripening: Late August to early September

Notes: Crabapple variety specifically grown for cider production

Roberts (Cider)

Taste: Sharp, acidic flavor perfect for cider blending. Adds tartness and complexity to cider blends.

Ripening: Mid to late September

Notes: Traditional cider apple variety, excellent for creating balanced cider blends

Blueberries

We have over 1000 blueberry bushes (and counting) across 3 acres with a relatively even split between our 3 planted varieties listed below:

Bluecrop

Taste: Classic blueberry flavor with good sweet-tart balance. Firm texture, excellent for fresh eating and baking.

Berry Size: Large berries

Ripening: Mid-July to early August

Notes: Most popular commercial variety, reliable producer

Duke

Taste: Sweet, mild flavor with low acid content. Very pleasant for fresh eating.

Berry Size: Large berries

Ripening: Early July (our earliest variety)

Notes: Early season variety, extends our harvest season

Elliot

Taste: Sweet with good flavor intensity. Firm berries that hold up well after picking.

Berry Size: Medium berries

Ripening: Late August to early September

Notes: Late season variety, extends harvest well into fall
